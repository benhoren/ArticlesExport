Asian stock markets rise as oil passes $50 a barrel
Asian share markets were higher as oil prices rose above $50 a barrel on speculation major producers are going to reduce output.
Crude prices have risen more than 20% since early August, meaning oil has swung back into a bull market.Japan's Nikkei rose 0.4% to close at 16,545.82 while the broader Topix also increased 0.4% to 1,295.67.Australia's S&P/ASX 200 closed 0.34% higher at 5,526.70 while New Zealand's NZX 50 gained 0.3% to 7,405.25.South Korea's Kospi index closed flat at 2,056.24.Shares in Samsung rose a further 2% having hit a record in the previous trading session. The world's largest smartphone maker saw its market value cross the $200bn mark on Thursday, making it five times more valuable than Japan's Sony.In Greater China, Hong Kong's Hang Seng reversed early gains to close 0.4% lower at 22,937.22, while the Shanghai Composite was little changed and closed flat at 3,108.10.
